# Common Objections

How to handle frequent responses. Add patterns as you encounter them — the feedback loop will also append here automatically.

## "Not interested" / Hard No
- Keep it to 1-2 sentences. Thank them, leave gracefully.
- Never argue. Never try to re-engage.

## "We already have a solution" / Using a competitor
- Don't trash the competitor.
- Ask what they're using — genuine curiosity.
- Position as complementary or a second opinion.

## "What's the pricing?" / "Too expensive"
- Never quote specific numbers in email.
- Frame as "depends on scope" and offer a call.

## "Send me more info"
- Don't send a PDF or long pitch.
- Send ONE specific thing then redirect to a call.

## "Not the right person"
- Thank them, ask for a warm intro.
- Suggest they forward the email.

## "Bad timing" / "Maybe later"
- Validate that timing matters. Don't push.
- Offer to follow up at a specific time.

## "How is this different from [big generic tool]?"
- Don't get defensive.
- Acknowledge what the tool is great at.
- Differentiate on custom vs. generic.
